# Character Creation Overview

## Creation Steps (High-Level)
1. **Choose a Race** – Drifter, Human, Shayan, or Hatchling.
2. **Choose an Archetype and Path** – True Mage, Dedicate, Scoundrel, Warrior, or Mercenary, each with specific paths.
3. **Assign Attributes** – Start from a baseline and spend points to customize.
4. **Select Catalyzation(s)** – Choose which aspects of magic your character has access to.
5. **Record Spells** – Determine how your character expresses magic and which spells they know.
6. **Assign Skill Points & Mastery** – Choose and rate skills; select one additional Mastery.
7. **Select Perks & Flaws** – Take up to 3 Flaws and an equal number of Perks (see dedicated Perks & Flaws cheat sheet).
8. **Purchase Defensives** – Allocate Brace, Dodge, Skepticism, and calculate hit points (see Defensives cheat sheet).
9. **Calculate Hit Points & Spell Pool** – Based on Fortitude, Will, and archetype bonuses.
10. **Purchase Equipment** – Spend starting credits on weapons, armor, gear, vehicles, or ships (see Equipment cheat sheet).

---

## Step 1 – Races
### Drifter
- **Attributes:** +1 Fortitude, +1 Agility.
- **Racial Ability – Cast Iron Stomach:** Immune to poisons and suffers no penalties from alcohol.

### Human
- **Attributes:** +1 to any one Attribute of choice, +1 Skepticism.
- **Racial Ability – Adaptable Skepticism:** The Skepticism bonus does **not** count toward the normal maximum for defenses.

### Shayan
- **Attributes:** +1 Reason, +1 Appearance, +1 Spell Pool.
- **Racial Ability – Delayed Aging:** Does not age normally; maintains appearance indefinitely as long as they regularly ingest magic.

### Hatchling
- **Attributes:** +2 Armor, +1 Fortitude.
- **Racial Ability – Breath Weapon:**
  - Aspect matches the hatchling’s magical aspect (fire, void, etc.).
  - Attack: **Ranged + Agility**, 5 base damage, **10‑meter cone**, resisted by target’s **Skepticism**.
  - Usable **once per scene** (or once per hour, at GM’s discretion on time scale).

## Step 2 – Archetypes & Paths
Each Archetype defines base magical capacity, hit points, initiative, and core skills; each Path adds masteries, extra skills, and special abilities.

### True Mage Archetype
- **Focus:** Knowledge and mastery of greater paths of magic.
- **Magic:** Must possess two adjacent aspects to unlock a **Greater Path** (see Magic & Circle of Eight, if available).
- **Starting Catalyzations:** 2 (huge advantage over other archetypes).
- **Base Stats:**
  - **Catalyzations:** 2
  - **Spell Pool:** 3
  - **Hit Points:** 10
  - **Initiative:** 0
- **Base Skills:**
  - Spellcasting 3
  - History & Lore 2
  - Arcana 2

#### Path of the Relic Hunter
- **Role:** Scholar‑adventurer and artifact specialist.
- **Masteries:** History & Lore, Perception, Arcana.
- **Extra Skill:** Perception 3.
- **Decipher:** Automatically understand and translate any written or spoken language as if it were native.
- **Tomb Robber:** Gains **Edge** on spells cast through, or attacks made with, magical devices such as spellstaves, spellpistols, and spellrifles.

#### Path of the Artificer
- **Role:** Magical engineer and crafter of weapons, armor, and devices.
- **Mastery:** Artificing.
- **Extra Skill:** Artificing 3.
- **Improvised Workshop:**
  - Can erect a portable workshop in **8 hours**, using an imbued focus linked to an extradimensional storage space.
  - Workshop does **not** provide a roll bonus, but **reduces crafting time by 20%**.
- **Invest Magic:**
  - May invest up to **Magic** points of spell pool into a single object via an 8‑hour ritual in a workshop.
  - Investment grants permanent bonuses (until reclaimed through another 8‑hour ritual):
    - **Armor:** +1 Armor per invested point.
    - **Weapon:** +1 Accuracy *or* +1 Damage per invested point.
  - If the artificer dies before reclaiming the investment, the enchantment becomes permanently fixed in the item.

#### Path of the Illusionist
- **Role:** Deception, disguise, and sensory manipulation.
- **Mastery:** Stealth.
- **Extra Skill:** Stealth 3.
- **Sustain Illusion:** May sustain **one illusion spell** without pledging any spell pool and without concentration.

#### Path of the Flame Reader
- **Role:** Diviner using fire and dream magic to read past, present, and possible futures.
- **Masteries:** Perception, Flame Reading.
- **Extra Skills:** Perception 3, Flame Reading 3.
- **Pierce All Veils:**
  - Gains **Edge** when attempting to see through illusions.
  - Automatically attempts to detect illusions even if unaware they exist.
  - Is always aware when being lied to.

#### Path of the Eradicator
- **Role:** Battle mage focused on mass destruction (fire and void).
- **Masteries:** Spellcasting, Ranged.
- **Extra Skill:** Ranged 3.
- **Efficient Killer:** Reduces the cost of all **Destruction, Fire, and Void** spells by **1** (minimum cost 1 unless a separate ability says otherwise).
- **Special:** Only Eradicators can cast **Disintegrate**, a feared spell that cannot be countered.

#### Path of the Summoner
- **Role:** Pact‑maker who calls forth bound allies from the umbral depths.
- **Masteries:** Spellcasting, Negotiation.
- **Extra Skill:** Negotiation 3.
- **Pact of the Defender:**
  - Has a standing pact with a powerful defender creature.
  - As a **standard action**, may summon this defender, which obeys commands.
  - Defender’s **magnitude ≤ Summoner’s Magic**; a new defender replaces any destroyed one.
- **Make Pact:**
  - May form a pact with any **willing** creature.
  - That creature can then be summoned as a standard action under agreed terms (often life debts or fear).

#### Path of the Necromancer
- **Role:** Binder of spirits to corpses and speaker to the dead.
- **Masteries:** Spellcasting, Negotiation.
- **Extra Skill:** Negotiation 3.
- **Animus:**
  - Possesses an animus (soul‑shard) they may place into a body (corpse or construct) as a **standard action**.
  - The animated body’s **magnitude = Necromancer’s Magic**.
  - Can recall the animus or move it to another body as a standard action.
  - When not animating a body, the animus resides in the necromancer’s shadow and can be spotted with a Perception check.

#### Path of the Shaman
- **Role:** Protective caster using Water and Spirit to ward allies and fortify bonds.
- **Masteries:** Spellcasting, Arcana.
- **Extra Skill:** Arcana 3.
- **Sustain Ward:** May sustain one **ward** without pledging spell pool and without concentration (after the initial casting).
- **Empower Wards:** Gains **Edge** on all spellcasting checks to create wards or other defensive magic.

#### Path of the Healer
- **Role:** Life and Water magic specialist focused on restoring allies.
- **Masteries:** Spellcasting, Medicine.
- **Extra Skill:** Medicine 3.
- **Empowered Healing:** Always has **Edge** when casting healing or restorative spells.
- **Compassionate Healer:** Reduces the cost of all **healing spells by 1** (minimum 0).

### Dedicate Archetype
- **Focus:** Devotion to a deity, Catalyst, or demigod that grants **Miracles** through a **Covenant**.
- **Paths:** Paladin, Soulcatcher.

#### Shared Dedicate Stats
- (Where not overridden by a path, the following apply.)
- **Typical Paladin/Soulcatcher Base Stats (Paladin block as example):**
  - **Catalyzations:** 1
  - **Spell Pool:** 2
  - **Hit Points:** 15
  - **Initiative:** 2
  - **Base Skills:** Ranged or Melee 3, Religion 2, Athletics 2 (per Paladin entry).

#### Path of the Paladin
- **Masteries:** Religion and either Ranged or Melee.
- **Covenant:**
  - Selects a Covenant with a deity or religion (from the Miracles & Covenants chapter or custom with GM).
  - Covenant determines available **Miracles**.
- **Miracles:** Starts with at least one Miracle from the chosen Covenant; may buy more with experience.
- **Hearty (Magic Resistance):** Gains **5 points of Magic Resistance**; enemy mages can **never have Edge** when casting spells against the Paladin.
- **Extra Skills:** Piloting 2, Navigation 2.
- **Starting Gear:** Inuran Mark III Spellarmor plus either a spellrifle or spellblade (GM’s choice).

#### Path of the Soulcatcher
- **Masteries:** Religion, Perception.
- **Extra Skills:** Perception 2.
- **Covenant:** Same rules as Paladin – chooses a deity/religion and gains Miracles tied to that Covenant.
- **Miracles:** Chooses **2 Miracles** from the Miracles & Covenants chapter; may purchase more with experience.
- **Claim Soul:**
  - If within **30 meters** of a magically‑active creature at its death, may spend the next action to capture either its **soul** or its **magical energy**, storing it in a **phylactery**.
  - Captured **magic** grants a permanent +1 Spell Pool; total Spell Pool gained this way cannot exceed **2 × Will**.
  - If the phylactery is destroyed, the soul is freed and the spell‑pool point from that soul is lost.
- **Unleash Soul:**
  - As an action, may crush a phylactery to unleash a vengeful spirit for a number of rounds equal to successes on a Magic + Religion roll.
  - The spirit’s power mirrors the original creature (as a spirit) and is **Vulnerable to Life**.
- **Deep Faith:** Once per day, may use **any Miracle one extra time**, even if its normal daily uses are exhausted.

### Scoundrel Archetype
- **Focus:** Assassins, thieves, and con artists relying on subtlety and opportunism.
- **Base Stats:**
  - **Catalyzations:** 1
  - **Spell Pool:** 3
  - **Hit Points:** 12
  - **Initiative:** 3
- **Base Skills:**
  - Stealth 3
  - Ranged 2
  - Perception 2
- **Paths:** Larcenist, Spellsniper.

#### Path of the Larcenist
- **Masteries:** Negotiation, Stealth, Larceny.
- **Extra Skills:** Negotiation 3, Larceny 3.
- **Disarm Device:** Gains **Edge** when opening locks or disarming traps/devices and can magically improvise tools for such tasks.
- **Aura of Disruption:** Difficult to record; cameras and scrying attempts frequently fail or mis‑target, making the Larcenist’s image hard to capture.
- **Silver Tongue:** Always has **Edge** on Negotiation checks and gains **+1 die** when using Negotiation to con or bluff.
- **Starting Gear:** Environmental armor, Mark V Spellpistol, and an extra **1,200 credits**.

#### Path of the Spellsniper
- **Masteries:** Ranged, Stealth.
- **Extra Skills:** Ranged 3, Athletics 3.
- **Snipe:**
  - If the target is unaware of the Spellsniper’s location, the attack gains **Edge (+2 successes)** and **+2 Accuracy**.
  - The attack hits something vital, dealing **+2 damage** of all involved types (physical and/or magical).
  - Applies to melee attacks as well, if the target is unaware.
- **One with Shadow:** Gains **Edge** on Stealth checks and may make a Stealth check during combat whenever they break line of sight.

---

### Warrior Archetype
- **Focus:** Front‑line combatants and pilots who combine tactics and raw lethality.
- **Paths:** War Mage, Tech Mage.

#### Path of the War Mage
- **Base Stats:**
  - **Catalyzations:** 1
  - **Spell Pool:** 2
  - **Hit Points:** 20
  - **Initiative:** 3
- **Base Skills:** Athletics 2; Melee or Ranged 3; Perception 2.
- **Masteries:** One of Melee or Ranged (player choice) and Athletics.
- **Devastating Strike:** When casting through a spellweapon (spellblade, spellrifle, etc.), the attack targets the lower of the enemy’s **Brace or Skepticism**, and the War Mage adds their **Magic** rating to damage.
- **Spell Riposte:**
  - Once per round, may attempt to parry a spell that directly targets them with a free **Melee + Agility** roll using a spellshield or spellblade.
  - Each success reduces the opponent’s successes; if reduced to 0, the spell is completely parried.
  - If the War Mage’s extra successes would also beat the caster’s Skepticism, the spell is **reflected back** at the caster.
- **Innate Spells:** May channel magic directly through their bodies rather than hard‑casting; note that without Spellcasting or an innate‑spells feature, a character cannot cast spells without a spellweapon or similar device.
- **Starting Gear:** A spellblade **or** spellrifle and minimal other possessions.

#### Path of the Tech Mage
- **Role:** Heavy combatant in spellarmor, blending tech and magic.
- **Starting Gear:** Inuran Mark V Spellrifle and **Mark V Standard Spellarmor**.
- **Mastery:** Either Ranged or Melee.
- **Come Get Some:** Gains **+1 Brace, +1 Dodge, and +1 Skepticism**; these bonuses **can exceed normal caps**.
- **Efficient Caster:** May reduce the cost of any spell by **1** (minimum 0), making low‑cost spells extremely spammable.

---

### Mercenary Archetype
- **Focus:** Non‑mages who rely on cyberware, firearms, and gear to compete with spellcasters.
- All Mercenaries possess a **cybernetic harness** wired into their nervous system, allowing modular implants and mission‑specific enhancements.
- **Base Stats:**
  - **Catalyzations:** 0
  - **Spell Pool:** 0
  - **Hit Points:** 20
  - **Initiative:** 3
- **Base Skills:** Melee 3, Ranged 3, Athletics 3, Perception 2.
- **Paths:** Mage Killer, Captain.

#### Path of the Mage Killer
- **Masteries:** Melee, Ranged.
- **Extra Skill:** Stealth 3.
- **Assassinate:** Gains **Edge on all attacks for a full round** if the target is unaware of the Mage Killer.
- **Arsenal (Cyberware Options – Choose 2 at Character Creation):**
  - **Stealth Generator:** Field that bends light and muffles sound; grants Edge on Stealth tests while active.
  - **Magic Inhibitor Skeleton:** Reinforced alloy skeleton that increases all spell DTs targeting the character (including healing) by **+2**.
  - **Cyber‑Senses:** Cybernetic eyes and ears; grants Edge on Perception checks and prevents being caught unaware.
  - **Augmented Reflexes I:** +1 Agility and +1 Dodge (can exceed racial maximum).
  - **Augmented Strength I:** +1 Strength and +1 Athletics (can exceed racial maximum).
  - **Reinforced Skeleton I:** +1 Fortitude and +1 Brace (can exceed racial maximum).
- **Starting Gear:** Mark VII Ternus Assault Rifle, 6 magazines of explosive rounds, 4 frag grenades, environmental body armor, and cybernetic spurs (4 base damage, 1 armor penetration).

#### Path of the Captain
- **Masteries:** Piloting, Negotiation.
- **Extra Skills:** Piloting 3, Negotiation 3.
- **Proof of Title:** Owns a Mark III fighter, corvette, or freighter (captain’s choice) worth several million credits; ship is genetically bonded to them and nearly impossible to steal.
- **Contacts:** Upon arriving at a new port or world, may make an **Intuition** check; on success, they know a useful contact there from past dealings.
- **Starting Gear:** Harry’s Pump Action Shotgun Mk V, 4 magazines of explosive rounds, NTM Cavalier Light body armor.

## Step 3 – Attributes
- Attributes range from **1 (Terrible)** to **5 (Peak Human)**; racial bonuses can raise the effective maximum above 5 for that attribute.
- All attributes start at **2**, and a standard character has **5 points** to distribute; no attribute may exceed 5 before racial bonuses.

### Attribute List and Uses
- **Reason:** Raw intelligence and analytical ability; important for problem‑solving and many technical skills.
- **Intuition:** Emotional intelligence, instincts, and social reading; used for Perception, Investigation, Religion, and some other skills.
- **Strength:** Physical power; affects melee damage and determines what class of armor can be worn.
- **Will:** Mental discipline and resilience; forms the basis of **Skepticism** and contributes **Will × 3** to Spell Pool.
- **Agility:** Reflexes and manual dexterity; used for **Dodge**, Ranged attacks, Stealth, Athletics, and many physical checks.
- **Appearance:** Physical attractiveness; sometimes used as a spell expression attribute and in social situations.
- **Charisma:** Force of personality and charm; key for Negotiation, Performance, and many social rolls.
- **Fortitude:** Physical endurance; forms the basis of **Brace** and contributes **Fortitude × 5** to hit points.
- ***Magic (Derived):*** Equals the number of Catalysts a character has visited; most start at 1, while True Mages start at 2. Magic above 5 marks a character as a demigod.

## Step 4 – Select Catalyzation
Each Catalyzation represents a specific connection to a divine source of magic. Unless otherwise stated, a Catalyzation grants **3 Spell Pool**, **3 resistance** to its aspect, and a **Bolt** spell.

### General Rules
- Non–True Mages normally choose **1 Catalyzation** at character creation; **True Mages choose 2**.
- The same aspect can be taken as straight **X Magic**, or via a special named Catalyzation for that aspect; you pick only **one option per aspect** you possess.
- The chosen aspects determine which spell lists you can draw from when selecting spells.

### Life Catalyzations
- **Life Magic:** Access to Life spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Life Resistance; add **Light Bolt** to lexicon.
- **Touch of Shaya:** Touch‑range heal; may spend any amount of Spell Pool to heal a target 1:1; also grants +3 Spell Pool and +3 Life Resistance.
- **Vanity of Shaya:** +1 Appearance, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Life Resistance.

### Air Catalyzations
- **Air Magic:** Access to Air spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Air Resistance; add **Lightning Bolt**.
- **Ascend to the Dragonflight:** Can fly at will at **45 kph**; maneuvering typically uses **Athletics + Agility**.
- **Grace of Virkonna:** +1 Agility, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Air Resistance.

### Dream Catalyzations
- **Dream Magic:** Access to Dream spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Dream Resistance; add **Dream Bolt**.
- **Vanish:** Once per day as an action, become **invisible**; cannot be directly targeted except by observers with some way to pierce invisibility; lasts until cancelled or the mage next sleeps.
- **Voice of Shi:** +1 Charisma, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Dream Resistance.

### Fire Catalyzations
- **Fire Magic:** Access to Fire spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Fire Resistance; add **Fire Bolt**.
- **Pierce All Veils (Fire version):** Once per day, may wreathe vision in divine flame to automatically see through illusions and perceive invisible targets; lasts until cancelled or until the mage sleeps (eyes glow with flame).
- **Strength of Van:** +1 Strength, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Fire Resistance.

### Void Catalyzations
- **Void Magic:** Access to Void spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Void Resistance; add **Void Bolt**.
- **Umbral Jaunt:** Up to **three times per day**, as an action, teleport through the umbral depths to any location within **30 meters**.
- **Xal’s Piercing Intellect:** +1 Reason, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Void Resistance.

### Earth Catalyzations
- **Earth Magic:** Access to Earth spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Earth Resistance; add **Acid Bolt**.
- **Resolve of Trakalon:** +1 Fortitude, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Earth Resistance.
- **Gift of Osmium:** Skin hardens to metal; gain **+3 Armor** and +3 Earth Resistance.

### Spirit Catalyzations
- **Spirit Magic:** Access to Spirit spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Spirit Resistance; add **Spirit Bolt**.
- **Simulacrum:**
  - Once per day as an action, create an incorporeal double at your location and shift your real body into the spirit world.
  - For up to **3 rounds**, you may move and act in ways that do not interact with the physical world; you can return early by choice.
  - The simulacrum is destroyed by a single attack but does not reveal your real location.
- **Resolve of Krox:** +1 Will, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Spirit Resistance.

### Water Catalyzations
- **Water Magic:** Access to Water spells; +3 Spell Pool; +3 Water Resistance; add **Frost Bolt**.
- **Marid’s Protection:** Gain **5 Magic Resistance**.
- **Marid’s Intuition:** +1 Intuition, +3 Spell Pool, +3 Water Resistance.

## Step 5 – Record Spells

### Spell Expression Attribute
- Each mage chooses one Attribute as their **magical expression**:
  - Intuition
  - Reason
  - Charisma
  - Appearance
- This attribute is used in **Spellcasting** rolls; once chosen, it **cannot be changed**.

### Bolt Spells and Modifiers
- Each aspect grants a basic **Bolt** spell (e.g., Fire Bolt, Void Bolt) used primarily to deal damage.
- Bolt spells may be modified (e.g., changed into a cone or radius effect) by paying additional Spell Pool, as long as the **total cost ≤ Magic**.
- Example size modifiers:
  - 3‑square/hex cone: +1 Spell Pool cost.
  - 4‑square/hex radius: +2 Spell Pool cost.

### Recording Starting Spells
- For each aspect your character has, add the corresponding **Bolt** spell to your sheet.
- Add **Manifest X** for each aspect (e.g., Manifest Fire, Manifest Void) to create minor, thematic magical effects of that element.
- If your archetype or path grants additional spells (e.g., True Mages), select them from the spell lists for aspects you possess.

## Step 6 – Skills and Mastery

### Skill Points and Ranks
- Characters start with **5 skill points** to assign during creation.
- No skill can start above **3 (Skilled/Professional)**.
- Skill checks roll **Attribute + Skill** d6; 5–6 are successes (4–6 if the skill is a Mastery).

### Masteries
- Some archetypes and paths grant specific skills as **Masteries**.
- Every character also chooses **one additional Mastery** in any skill.
- When rolling a Mastery, dice showing **4–6** count as successes instead of only 5–6.

### Skill Rank Scale
- Rank 1 – **Untrained**
- Rank 2 – **Basic Training**
- Rank 3 – **Skilled / Professional**
- Rank 4 – **Veteran**
- Rank 5 – **Master**

### Skill List
- **Active Skills:** Ranged, Melee, Unarmed, Spellcasting.
- **Technical / General Skills:** Warfare, Artificing, Negotiation, Larceny, Religion, Investigation, Hacking, Linguistics, Performance, Crafting, Stealth, Engineering, Piloting, Gunnery, Athletics, Perception, Arcana, History & Lore, Flame Reading, First Aid, Navigation.

### Skill Descriptions (Abridged)
- **Ranged (Agility):** Use of pistols, spellpistols, rifles, spellrifles, and other ranged weapons.
- **Melee (Strength):** Use of melee weapons (swords, axes, staves, etc.).
- **Unarmed (Strength):** Fighting without weapons.
- **Spellcasting (Expression Attribute):** Casting spells not mediated by spellrifles, spellships, etc.
- **Engineering (Reason):** Operating, troubleshooting, and designing technology.
- **Piloting (Agility):** Piloting spellships, spellfighters, and conventional craft.
- **Gunnery (Agility):** Operating vehicle and ship weapons, including spellcannons for mages.
- **Athletics (Fortitude/Strength/Agility):** Running, climbing, acrobatics, extended exertion.
- **Perception (Intuition):** Spotting danger, noticing details, and active searching.
- **Arcana (Reason):** Knowledge of magic, spells, enchantments, eldimagi, and gods from a magical perspective.
- **History & Lore (Reason):** Historical and esoteric scientific or cultural knowledge.
- **Flame Reading (Charisma):** Fire‑based divination (requires Fire magic, and Dream + Fire for advanced use).
- **First Aid (Reason):** Treating basic wounds, infections, and simple diseases.
- **Navigation (Will):** Plotting courses in normal space and the Umbral Depths.
- **Religion (Intuition):** Knowledge of rituals, theology, and communing with deities; often used for Miracles.
- **Investigation (Reason/Intuition):** Piecing together clues and solving mysteries.
- **Negotiation (Charisma):** Making deals, reaching agreements, haggling (each success beyond the opponent’s reduces price by 5%).
- **Larceny (Agility):** Theft, pickpocketing, cracking safes, forging documents.
- **Hacking (Reason):** Infiltrating and manipulating computer systems.
- **Linguistics (Reason):** Knowing and learning languages; can also decipher unknown tongues over time.
- **Performance (Varies):** Any kind of performance (oration, music, stunts); attribute depends on performance type.
- **Stealth (Agility):** Remaining unseen; Stealth successes become the DT for Perception checks to detect you.
- **Warfare (Reason/Intuition):** Tactical and strategic planning for battles and wars.
- **Crafting (Agility/Strength):** Physically crafting items (carpentry, smithing, etc.); can complement Engineering.
- **Artificing (Reason/Intuition):** Working with magical materials and creating magic items (best combined with Enchanting).
